Mastering the Metrics

Understanding financial ratios is critical to making robust investment decisions. Click on any card below to flip it and view its formula, definition, and why it plays a major role in FinApp's decision engine.

1

ROE

Return on Equity

Click to flip

Formula

Net Income / Shareholders' Equity

Why it matters

Measures how efficiently a company uses investors' money to generate profits. In FinApp, ROE heavily influences the Dividend and Quality pillars. A high, consistent ROE indicates a strong economic moat.

2

PER

Price to Earnings Ratio

Click to flip

Formula

Stock Price / Earnings Per Share (EPS)

Why it matters

Shows how much you are paying for $1 of earnings. A lower PER means the stock is cheaper. Used in FinApp's valuation models to find undervalued opportunities before they rebound.

3

PBV

Price to Book Value

Click to flip

Formula

Stock Price / Book Value Per Share

Why it matters

Compares market valuation to actual net assets. Crucial for Banking and Financial stocks where assets are liquid. FinApp exponentially weights PBV for banking sectors over Graham's Number.

4

DER

Debt to Equity Ratio

Click to flip

Formula

Total Liabilities / Total Equity

Why it matters

A primary solvency indicator. High DER (>200%) warns of toxic debt leading to potential bankruptcy. FinApp uses this in the Solvency Quality Gate to downgrade risky 'cheap' stocks to NO BUY.

5

MACD

Moving Average Convergence

Click to flip

Formula

12-Period EMA minus 26-Period EMA

Why it matters

A trend-following momentum indicator. Used by traders to spot market entry/exit points when the MACD crosses its signal line, revealing short-term momentum shifts.

6

RSI

Relative Strength Index

Click to flip

Formula

100 - (100 / (1 + (Avg Gain / Avg Loss)))

Why it matters

Measures the speed and magnitude of recent price changes. RSI > 70 implies Overbought (expensive), while RSI < 30 implies Oversold (cheap). Perfect for timing entries based on the Discount Score.

7

MOS

Margin of Safety

Click to flip

Formula

1 - (Current Price / Fair Value)

Why it matters

The ultimate risk-mitigator. It demands purchasing a stock significantly below its estimated intrinsic value (Graham's Number or PBV rules). FinApp uses MOS to determine the baseline Discount Score.

8

Quality
Score

Quality Score

Click to flip

Formula

Weighted Sum(ROE, PBV vs Industry, Solvency)

Why it matters

A multi-factor algorithmic rating (0-100) assessing a company's fundamental strength based on ROE, PBV efficiency, and Solvency. Think of it as FinApp's corporate health check.

9

Discount
Score

Discount Score

Click to flip

Formula

Normalization_Curve(Margin of Safety)

Why it matters

A rating (0-100) determining how cheap the stock is relative to its Fair Value. The deeper the Margin of Safety, the closer the Discount Score approaches a perfect 100.

10

DPR

Dividend Payout Ratio

Click to flip

Formula

Total Dividends / Net Income

Why it matters

The proportion of earnings paid out as dividends. FinApp penalizes ratios > 85% as 'unsafe' unless offset by aggressive historical dividend growth, protecting the Yield Score.

Investment Strategies & Categories

FinApp categorizes stocks into distinct playstyles and applies strict quality gates. Understanding these concepts helps clarify why a stock might be downgraded despite looking cheap.

Sector Processing

Different industries require different valuation metrics. FinApp specifically modifies Margin of Safety processing for Financials (favoring PBV) versus Tech (ignoring Graham's Number due to intangible assets).

Risk Management Gate

A core FinApp penalty system. Even if a stock passes valuation tests, it will be downgraded to HOLD or NO BUY if it exhibits toxic debt-to-equity ratios (>350%) or poor liquidity (Current Ratio < 1.0).

Bluechip
